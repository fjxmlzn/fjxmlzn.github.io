Research Projects

Large Scale Automatic Sybil Attacks and Vulnerability Measurement on Mobile Services

Nowadays many mobile services have integrated crowdsourcing techniques, which collect information and ideas from its online users rather than from employees or suppliers, and then present the results back to users. These services provide us with much convenience, while they are inherently vulnerable to sybil attacks - creating a large number of fake users to disrupt the system. The goal of our project is to generalize and scale up the sybil attack techniques and replace the work by automatic scripts. In this way, we can measure how many apps are exposed to those attacks and how the others defend against those attacks in a large scale manner.
Bolun Wang, Zinan Lin, Selena Huang, Xinyi Zhang, Gang Wang, Ben Y. Zhao

MOT+: A Universal Platform for Multi-Object Tracking

Multi-object tracking plays an important role in many computer vision tasks. Within the multi-object algorithm framework, there are many topics that researchers focus on, such as the similarity measure and data association. Nowadays, if researchers want to test a new algorithm on a tiny focus, they need to implement the whole multi-object tracking framework. This kind of process has several drawbacks: 1) It slows down the processes of developing and testing new algorithms. 2) Because of the different implementations in the rest part of the framework, it is difficult to fairly compare results of works on the same topic. 3) Different implementation patterns make it hard for other researchers to use the code on further research. To address the problems above, I create a universal platform called MOT+ in which researchers can design, test and evaluate multi-object tracking algorithms easily and efficiently and users can construct a desired tracker just by simple configurations. Furthermore, I provide a reconstructed version of CMOT algorithm and a sample GUI program using MOT+.
Zinan Lin, Qi Guo, Le Dan, Xiangyang Ji

Advanced Multi-Step Optimal Donation Strategy Based on Objective Criteria of Gaussian Distribution and Prediction Model of Neural Network

If a charity wants to donate a large amount of money to schools, what's the best donation strategy so that students will get the most benefit? In the paper, we analyse this problem. First, we propose Relative Institution Quality (RIQ) for evaluating school's teaching quality based on Gaussian Distance Measurement (GDM). Second, we use neural network to find the relationship between the amount of donation to a school and the positive effect on its students. Third, we prove that finding an optimal strategy that achieves the most benefit among schools is a Strict Non-Deterministic Polynomial (S-NPC) problem and give an approximate optimal solution. We won the Meritorious Winner in COMAP’s 2016 Mathematical Contest in Modeling.
Zinan Lin, Xinyu Jin, Shengjie Lin

A Two-Step Model for Searching a Lost Plane

Searching for a lost plane in vast ocean became an urgent task in the past few years. In this article, we deeply analyse this problem. We divide the problem into two steps. In the first step we focus on how to get a continuous probability density distribution which describes the possible point where the plane crashed. In the second step we focus on how to find an adaptive and effective rescue plan using the probability density distribution. We won the Meritorious Winner in COMAP’s 2015 Mathematical Contest in Modeling.
Zinan Lin, Renzhi Duan, Mengying Xu
Details PDF

Image Similarity Measure Based on Image Sparse Representation

In recent years, sparse representation of images has been widely used in image denoising scenes. Inspired by its excellent performance of extracting image's local structure, I am eager to see whether it will work in image similarity. In this project, I proposed an image similarity measure which is based on image's sparse representation. And I tested its performance in image classification and clustering.
Zinan Lin, Yuantao Gu
